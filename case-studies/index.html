Technical Case Studies & Writing Samples

Each sample below highlights a specific business friction, my reverse-engineering approach, and the final user impact.

📄 How do I install VNC Viewer and VNC Server?​

  • The Business Problem: Cross-platform remote connection configurations were confusing for distributed employees, spiking helpdesk triage tickets.
  • My Solution: Formulated a deployment-ready guide mapping out client and server setups clearly across enterprise operating systems.
  • The Impact: Established a clear baseline configuration standard, deflecting trivial cross-platform connection issues.

View VNC Setup Guide (PDF)

📄 How do I install Office 365 apps?​

  • The Business Problem: The internal IT helpdesk was being flooded with repetitive tickets from non-technical staff requesting software installations, killing engineering bandwidth.
  • My Solution: Designed a friction-free, self-service guide explicitly written for low-technical literacy users to empower autonomous installation.
  • The Impact: Successfully lowered operational bottlenecks and accelerated autonomous workforce onboarding times.

View Office 365 Setup Guide (PDF)

📄 Can I make my Project Private? (FeedBear.com)​

  • The Business Problem: Users navigating evaluation feedback loops were confused about data privacy options, leading to account creation anxiety and trial drop-offs.
  • My Solution: Working with a basic trial account and minimal visual assets, I reverse-engineered the layout behavior to map out an explicit project privacy guide.
  • The Impact: Improved critical feature discovery and enhanced user confidence during product evaluation cycles.

View Privacy Configuration Guide (PDF)

📄 How do I upgrade to a higher plan? (FeedBear.com)​

  • The Business Problem: Users trying to scale their internal feedback loops couldn't easily locate tier differences, introducing friction to the self-service expansion pipeline.
  • My Solution: Mapped out a highly transparent walkthrough covering plan navigation, monthly/annual toggles, and corporate upgrade request flows.
  • The Impact: Streamlined account scaling and minimized drop-offs during checkout workflows.
