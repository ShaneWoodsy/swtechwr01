I looked through an old presentation I created years ago. I immediately felt appreciation for my past experiences. In 2017, I was tasked by my IT director to research five new things that I did not know of and present my findings to my fellow IT team members.

Of course, I cringed at the thought of standing in front of a room and speaking about something I was not fully versed in. What I did not know was that this simple task prepared me for my future.
{/* truncate */}
Many people have asked me “how can I get into IT?” Curiosity and the ability to take the initiative, research many topics, and find the appropriate information will take you very far. Curiosity has always felt like an advantage.

Naturally, I am a creative person who enjoys being given a blank canvas and asked to come up with anything of my choosing to present. Back in school, I can remember being overjoyed when I arrived in my creative writing class to free write on any topic of our choosing. I also enjoyed writing about specific topics that were required for the course as well.

The five things project I was given years ago allowed me to think outside of the box and present things that some of the senior IT leadership may have known or were not aware of.

The benefit for me was that I wanted to use this opportunity to not only prove to my teammates that I took the task seriously, but also that I could share some of my personality with my teammates.

My 5 Things Project
When I sat down to draft my presentation I considered the following:

Who is my target audience?
What information do I want to share?
How do I want to present the information?
What is my deadline for completing the work?
Finally, how can I add a little of my personality to the presentation?
I designed the presentation to start with a familiar sight and sound to catch my viewer's attention.

To make a long story short, I enjoyed the process of creating the project. 😀

Back then I did not consider how some of the elements of my assignment such as defining my target audience, and what information could be useful in other roles. While I ramp up to transition my career into technical writing I am enjoying the journey and look forward to continued learning.

Initially, I thought that my teammates would not be interested in the content I shared. They were interested, particularly in how to play around with developer tools.

In my opinion, it helps to work in an environment where your leadership encourages you to step outside of your comfort zone. What initially made me cringe with nervousness, gave me the confidence I needed to present information to customers, clients, and leaders. I am grateful for this experience.
